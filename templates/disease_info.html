Know your plants - Plant Diseases Information

Disease 1: Powdery Mildew

Symptoms: White powdery substance on leaves, stems, and flowers.

Precautions:
-Ensure proper air circulation by spacing plants appropriately.
-Avoid overhead watering, as moisture on leaves can promote the growth of powdery mildew.
-Apply fungicides early in the season as a preventive measure.

Cure:
-Remove and destroy affected plant parts.
-Apply fungicides containing sulfur or neem oil.
-Prune plants to improve air circulation.

Disease 2: Late Blight

Symptoms: Dark spots on leaves, wilting, and rapid deterioration of plant health.

Precautions:
-Use disease-resistant plant varieties.
-Water plants at the base to avoid wetting foliage.
-Apply copper-based fungicides preventively.

Cure:
-Remove and destroy infected plant material.
-Apply fungicides containing copper or chlorothalonil.
-Rotate crops to prevent the buildup of pathogens in the soil.

Diease 3: Apple Cedar Rust

Symptoms: Early symptoms on apple trees include yellow spots on the upper surfaces of the leaves. As the disease progresses, these spots enlarge and bright orange-yellow lesions form on the undersides of the leaves.
Precautions:
-Plant resistant apple varieties if cedar-apple rust is common in your area.
-Avoid planting Eastern red cedars near apple trees to prevent cross-infection.
-Regularly inspect trees for the presence of galls on cedars or leaf lesions on apples, especially in the spring.
Cure:
-Apply fungicides labeled for use against cedar-apple rust during the spring, following the product label's instructions for timing and application rates.
-Prune and dispose of any affected parts of the apple tree to reduce the amount of fungal spores that could spread the disease.

Disease 4: Corn Common Rust

Symptoms:Early symptoms include light yellow spots on the upper surfaces of leaves.
Precautions:
-Plant rust-resistant corn varieties if common rust is a known issue in the area.
-Crop rotation can help reduce the fungal spores in the soil.
-Avoid planting corn after corn, as this can encourage the survival and buildup of rust spores.
Cure:
-Fungicides can be effective, particularly if applied when rust first appears and before it becomes severe.
-If rust appears late in the season or after the silking stage, fungicide application may not be economical.

Disease 5: Tomato Yellow Curl Virus

Symptoms:Leaves of infected plants show a characteristic yellowing and curling.
Precautions:
-Use virus-free seeds and transplants.
-Implement strict vector control, as the virus is most commonly spread by whiteflies.
-Use insect netting or reflective mulches to prevent whiteflies from reaching the plants.
Cure:
-There is no cure once plants are infected with TYLCV.
-Manage whiteflies with insecticides or biological controls, following the label instructions carefully.
-Crop rotation and field sanitation can help reduce the likelihood of future infections.

Disease 6: Apple scab

Symptoms: Fungal spots on leaves and fruit, leading to defoliation and reduced fruit quality.
Cause: The fungus (Venturia inaequalis) overwinters in fallen leaves and infects new growth in spring.
Cure: Use fungicides like captan or mancozeb, plant resistant apple varieties like 'Liberty' or 'Freedom', and practice good sanitation by removing fallen leaves.

Disease 7: Apple Black rot

Symptoms: Circular brown lesions on fruit and leaves, leading to mummification.
Cause: The fungus (Botryosphaeria obtusa) infects through wounds or natural openings.
Cure: Apply fungicides like thiophanate-methyl or pyraclostrobin, remove and destroy infected fruit, and prune branches.

Disease 8: Cherry Powdery mildew

Symptoms: White powdery growth on leaves, affecting cherry trees.
Cause: Fungus (Podosphaera clandestina) thrives in humid conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like sulfur or potassium bicarbonate, and ensure proper tree spacing for air circulation.

Disease 9: Corn Cercospora leaf spot & Gray leaf spot

Symptoms: Brownish lesions on leaves, affecting corn yield.
Cause: Fungi (Cercospora spp. & Cercospora zeae-maydis) infect corn leaves during warm, humid conditions.
Cure: Apply fungicides like azoxystrobin or pyraclostrobin before symptoms appear. Implementing a crop rotation with non-host crops can help reduce disease pressure.

Disease 10: Corn Northern Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Cigar-shaped lesions on leaves, reducing yield.
Cause: Fungus (Exserohilum turcicum) thrives in cool, humid conditions.
Cure: Apply fungicides like azoxystrobin or propiconazole. Plant resistant corn varieties and practice crop rotation.

Disease 11: Grape Black rot

Symptoms: Brown lesions on leaves and fruit, leading to fruit decay.
Cause: Fungus (Guignardia bidwellii) infects grapes during wet weather.
Cure: Apply fungicides like mancozeb or thiophanate-methyl. Prune vines for better air circulation.

Disease 12: Grape Esca (Black Measles)

Symptoms: Vine decline, black streaks in the wood.
Cause: Combination of fungi and bacteria (Phaeomoniella chlamydospora, etc.).
Cure: Apply fungicides like propiconazole or tebuconazole during pruning cuts and before bud break. Pruning during dry periods and using pruning paste can help prevent infection.

Disease 13: Grape Leaf blight

Symptoms: Brown spots on leaves, affecting grapevines.
Cause: Fungus (Isariopsis spp.) infects grape leaves during warm, wet conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like azoxystrobin or pyraclostrobin. Prune vines and remove infected leaves.

Disease 14: Peach Bacterial spot

Symptoms: Dark spots on leaves and fruit, cankers on twigs.
Cause: Bacteria (Xanthomonas arboricola pv. pruni) infects through wounds or natural openings.
Cure: Use copper-based fungicides or streptomycin. Plant resistant peach varieties and prune to improve air circulation.

Disease 15: Pepper, bell Bacterial spot

Symptoms: Dark lesions on leaves and fruit, affecting bell peppers.
Cause: Bacteria (Xanthomonas spp.) infects pepper plants through wounds or natural openings.
Cure: Apply copper-based fungicides or streptomycin, plant resistant pepper varieties.

Disease 16: Potato Early blight

Symptoms: Dark spots on leaves, leading to defoliation.
Cause: Fungus (Alternaria solani) survives in plant debris and soil.
Cure: Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil or mancozeb. Rotate crops with non-host plants, remove infected plant debris.

Disease 17: Potato Late blight

Symptoms: Dark lesions on leaves, leading to rapid foliage collapse.
Cause: Fungus (Phytophthora infestans) thrives in cool, wet conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like mefenoxam or mancozeb, plant resistant potato varieties like 'Kennebec' or 'Russet Burbank'.

Disease 18: Tomato Bacterial spot

Symptoms: Dark lesions with yellow halos on leaves and fruit.
Cause: Bacteria (Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria) thrives in warm, humid conditions.
Cure: Apply copper-based fungicides or streptomycin, plant resistant tomato varieties.

Disease 19: Tomato Early blight

Symptoms: Brown spots on lower leaves, affecting tomato plants.
Cause: Fungus (Alternaria solani) survives in plant debris and soil.
Cure: Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil or mancozeb. Rotate crops with non-host plants, remove infected plant debris.

Disease 20: Tomato Late blight

Symptoms: Dark lesions on leaves, leading to rapid foliage collapse.
Cause: Fungus (Phytophthora infestans) thrives in cool, wet conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like mefenoxam or mancozeb, plant resistant tomato varieties like 'Mountain Merit' or 'Defiant'.

Disease 21: Tomato Leaf Mold

Symptoms: Yellow patches on upper leaves with a velvety texture underneath.
Cause: Fungus (Fulvia fulva) thrives in warm, humid conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like chlorothalonil or mancozeb, ensure good ventilation and spacing between plants.

Disease 22: Tomato Septoria leaf spot

Symptoms: Dark spots with light centers on lower leaves.
Cause: Fungus (Septoria lycopersici) infects during warm, wet conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like chlorothalonil or mancozeb, remove infected leaves, practice crop rotation.

Disease 23: Tomato Spider mites

Symptoms: Stippling on leaves due to mite feeding.
Cause: Mites (Tetranychus spp.) thrive in hot, dry conditions.
Cure: Use insecticidal soaps or neem oil, introduce predatory mites like Phytoseiulus persimilis, maintain high humidity levels.

Disease 24: Tomato Target Spot

Symptoms: Target-like spots on leaves.
Cause: Fungus (Corynespora cassiicola) infects during warm, wet conditions.
Cure: Use fungicides like chlorothalonil or mancozeb, remove infected leaves, ensure good air circulation.

Disease 25: Tomato Tomato mosaic virus

Symptoms: Mottling, distortion, stunted growth.
Cause: Virus (ToMV) transmitted through contaminated tools, hands, or sap.
Cure: No cure available. Use virus-free seed, avoid touching plants when wet, disinfect tools between plants, plant resistant tomato varieties like 'Mountain Fresh Plus' or 'Big Beef'.
